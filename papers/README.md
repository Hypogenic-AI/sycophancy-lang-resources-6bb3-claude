# Downloaded Papers

## Sycophancy in LLMs

1. **Towards Understanding Sycophancy in Language Models** (2310.13548_sycophancy_understanding.pdf)
   - Authors: Sharma, Tong, Korbak, et al. (Anthropic)
   - Year: 2023 (ICLR 2024)
   - arXiv: 2310.13548
   - Why relevant: Foundational sycophancy measurement paper. Defines four sycophancy types, provides SycophancyEval benchmark. No multilingual evaluation -- the gap we fill.

2. **Sycophancy in Large Language Models: Causes and Mitigations** (2411.15287_sycophancy_causes_mitigations.pdf)
   - Authors: Malmqvist
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2411.15287
   - Why relevant: Comprehensive survey of causes (RLHF, training data) and mitigations. English-only.

3. **ELEPHANT: Measuring Social Sycophancy in LLMs** (2505.13995_elephant_social_sycophancy.pdf)
   - Authors: Cheng, Yu, Lee, Khadpe, Ibrahim, Jurafsky
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2505.13995
   - Why relevant: Social sycophancy (face-preserving behavior). LLMs preserve face 45pp more than humans.

## Bullshit Detection and Factual Accuracy

4. **Machine Bullshit: Characterizing the Emergent Disregard for Truth in LLMs** (2507.07484_machine_bullshit.pdf)
   - Authors: Liang, Hu, Zhao, Song, Griffiths, Fisac
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2507.07484
   - Why relevant: Bullshit Index metric, BullshitEval benchmark. Sycophancy as bullshit subtype. RLHF exacerbates bullshit.

## Multilingual LLM Performance

5. **Language Ranker: Quantifying LLM Performance Across Languages** (2404.11553_language_ranker.pdf)
   - Authors: Li, Shi, Liu, Yang, Payani, Liu, Du
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2404.11553
   - Why relevant: Methodology for quantifying language resource level via pre-training data proportion. Up to 24.3% performance decline in low-resource languages.

6. **MMLU-ProX: Multilingual Benchmark for Advanced LLM Evaluation** (2503.10497_mmlu_prox.pdf)
   - Authors: Xuan et al.
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2503.10497
   - Why relevant: 29-language benchmark enabling cross-linguistic LLM capability comparison.

7. **Aya 23: Open Weight Releases for Multilingual Progress** (2405.15032_aya23.pdf)
   - Authors: Aryabumi et al. (Cohere For AI)
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2405.15032
   - Why relevant: Open multilingual model (23 languages) as potential test subject.

## MKQA Dataset

8. **MKQA: A Linguistically Diverse Benchmark for Multilingual Open Domain QA** (2007.15207_mkqa.pdf)
   - Authors: Longpre, Lu, Daiber (Apple)
   - Year: 2020 (TACL 2021)
   - arXiv: 2007.15207
   - Why relevant: 10K QA pairs across 26 languages. Parallel, language-independent answers. Ideal for creating multilingual sycophancy test sets.

## Multilingual Safety and Alignment

9. **The Language Barrier: Dissecting Safety Challenges of LLMs in Multilingual Contexts** (2401.13136_language_barrier_safety.pdf)
   - Authors: Shen, Tan, Chen, et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2401.13136
   - Why relevant: Core evidence that alignment fails for low-resource languages. 35% harmful rate for low-resource vs 1% for high-resource on GPT-4.

10. **The State of Multilingual LLM Safety Research** (2505.24119_multilingual_safety_survey.pdf)
    - Authors: Yong, Ermis, Fadaee, Bach, Kreutzer
    - Year: 2025
    - arXiv: 2505.24119
    - Why relevant: Comprehensive survey. Most safety data is English/Chinese-centric.

11. **Multilingual Jailbreak Challenges in LLMs** (2310.06474_multilingual_jailbreak.pdf)
    - Authors: Deng, Zhang, Pan, Bing
    - Year: 2023 (ICLR 2024)
    - arXiv: 2310.06474
    - Why relevant: Low-resource languages have ~3x higher harmful content likelihood.

12. **Low-Resource Languages Jailbreak GPT-4** (2310.02446_low_resource_jailbreak.pdf)
    - Authors: Yong, Menghini, Bach
    - Year: 2023
    - arXiv: 2310.02446
    - Why relevant: Strongest quantitative evidence: LRL-Combined 79% bypass vs HRL-Combined 11%.

13. **Multilingual Blending: LLM Safety Alignment with Language Mixture** (2407.07342_multilingual_blending.pdf)
    - Authors: Song, Huang, Zhou, Ma
    - Year: 2024
    - arXiv: 2407.07342
    - Why relevant: Language mixing bypasses safety. Linguistic features affect bypass effectiveness.
